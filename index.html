South indian food

Dosas: paper-thin crepes

A traditional breakfast food made of fermented rice-and-lentil batter, a dosa is much like a crispy thin crepe. Its accompanied by sambar (a hot lentil soup) and coconut chutney.Masala dosas are stuffed with a spicy mash of potato and onion; plain dosas are hollow; rava dosas are made from semolina .

Idlis: steamed rice cakes

Soft, fluffy and ivory-coloured, idlis are what many South Indian families eat for breakfast. A fermented lentil-and rice-batter is steamed in little circular moulds, and the resulting spherical rice cakes are served with sambar and chutney.

Dal bhat

Dal bhat is a traditional meal from the Indian subcontinent. It consists of steamed rice and a cooked lentil soup called dal. It is a staple food in these countries. Bhat or Chawal means "boiled rice" in a number of Indo-Aryan languages.

Dal baati

Dal baati is an Indian dish of dal (lentils) and baati (hard wheat rolls).It is popular in Maharashtra's Khandesh and Vidarbha region, Rajasthan, Gujarat, Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh (especially in Braj, Nimar and Malwa regions) .Dal is prepared using tuvaar dal, chana dall etc. Baati is a hard bread made up of wheat flour commonly known as aata. Wheat flour is kneaded with little bit of salt, dahi (yogurt) and water.
